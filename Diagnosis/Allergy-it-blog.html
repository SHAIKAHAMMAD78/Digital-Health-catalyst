Intradermal Test

What is an Intradermal Test?

The Intradermal Test is a diagnostic method used to detect allergies by injecting small amounts of allergens into the skin.

How Does It Work?

A small amount of allergen is injected into the dermis layer of the skin to observe any allergic reaction.

Why is It Important?

This test is especially useful for identifying allergies that may not be detected by a standard skin prick test.

Benefits of Intradermal Test

Provides a more accurate allergy diagnosis and helps tailor personalized treatment plans.

How to Prepare

Avoid antihistamines before the test and inform your doctor about any medications you take.

Understanding Results

Results are typically available within 15-30 minutes, helping doctors determine the best course of action.
