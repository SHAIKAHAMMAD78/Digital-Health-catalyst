PCOS

Overview

PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome) is diagnosed through a combination of medical history, physical exams, and tests. Key tests include blood tests to measure hormone levels, check for insulin resistance, and rule out other conditions like thyroid disorders. A pelvic ultrasound is often used to detect multiple cysts on the ovaries, a common sign of PCOS. Doctors may also assess symptoms like irregular periods, excess hair growth, and acne. Diagnosis typically follows the Rotterdam Criteria, requiring two of three indicators: irregular periods, elevated androgens, or polycystic ovaries. Early diagnosis helps manage symptoms and prevent complications.
