What is Endoscopy Testing?

Endoscopy is a medical procedure that uses a flexible tube with a camera to examine internal organs. Common types include Upper GI Endoscopy (for the stomach and esophagus), Colonoscopy (for the large intestine), Bronchoscopy (for the lungs), Cystoscopy (for the bladder), and Hysteroscopy (for the uterus). It helps diagnose conditions like ulcers, tumors, infections, and digestive issues. The procedure is minimally invasive and often done under sedation. It is widely used for early detection and treatment of various diseases.
