Pulmonary Health Risks

What Are Pulmonary Health Risks?

Pulmonary health risks refer to conditions affecting the lungs and respiratory system, leading to breathing difficulties and reduced oxygen supply to the body.

Common Pulmonary Diseases

  • Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD): A progressive lung disease that causes airflow blockage and breathing problems.
  • Asthma: A condition where the airways narrow and swell, making breathing difficult.
  • Pneumonia: A lung infection that causes inflammation and fluid accumulation in the air sacs.
  • Pulmonary Fibrosis: A lung disease that causes scarring, making it difficult to breathe.
  • Pulmonary Hypertension: High blood pressure in the lungs that strains the heart.
  • Emphysema: A type of COPD that damages air sacs, reducing oxygen exchange.

Causes & Risk Factors

  • Smoking and exposure to air pollutants
  • Genetic predisposition
  • Occupational exposure to harmful chemicals
  • Respiratory infections
  • Chronic inflammation and autoimmune diseases

Symptoms to Watch For

  • Chronic cough with mucus
  • Shortness of breath
  • Wheezing and chest tightness
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Frequent lung infections
  • Blue tint to lips or fingernails (due to low oxygen)

Prevention & Management

  • Avoid smoking and exposure to pollutants
  • Maintain a healthy lifestyle with regular exercise
  • Follow prescribed medications and treatments
  • Practice breathing exercises and therapy
  • Seek medical advice for persistent symptoms
