Health Conditions Caused by Blood Disorders

Anemia

Anemia is a condition where the blood lacks sufficient red blood cells or hemoglobin, leading to fatigue, weakness, and pale skin. Severe anemia can cause heart problems and cognitive issues.

Leukemia

Leukemia is a type of cancer affecting blood and bone marrow, leading to an abnormal production of white blood cells. It can weaken the immune system, making the body vulnerable to infections.

Hemophilia

Hemophilia is a rare disorder where blood does not clot properly due to the lack of clotting factors, leading to excessive bleeding. Even minor injuries can result in prolonged bleeding, and internal bleeding can cause severe complications.

Sickle Cell Disease

A genetic disorder where red blood cells become misshapen, causing blockages in blood vessels, pain, and circulation problems. It can lead to organ damage, infections, and strokes.

Blood Clots (Thrombosis)

Blood clots can lead to life-threatening conditions such as deep vein thrombosis (DVT) and pulmonary embolism. If a clot travels to the lungs, heart, or brain, it can result in strokes, heart attacks, or respiratory failure.

Polycythemia Vera

A rare blood disorder where the body produces too many red blood cells, increasing blood viscosity and raising the risk of clots, strokes, and heart attacks.

Thalassemia

A genetic disorder where the body produces an abnormal form of hemoglobin, leading to reduced oxygen transport. It can cause severe anemia, bone deformities, and developmental issues.

Sepsis

Sepsis is a life-threatening condition caused by the body's extreme response to an infection in the blood. It can lead to organ failure, shock, and even death if not treated promptly.

Multiple Myeloma

Multiple myeloma is a cancer of plasma cells that affects bone marrow, weakening the bones and leading to frequent fractures, kidney damage, and suppressed immunity.
