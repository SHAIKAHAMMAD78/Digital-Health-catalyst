Tumor Markers

What are Tumor Markers?

Tumor markers are substances found in blood, urine, or tissues that may indicate the presence of cancer.

How Do They Work?

These markers are produced by cancer cells or by the body in response to cancer and are measured through lab tests.

Why are They Important?

Tumor markers help in cancer diagnosis, monitoring treatment response, and detecting recurrence.

Benefits of These Tests

They assist in early cancer detection, guide treatment decisions, and improve patient outcomes.

How to Prepare

Fasting may be required for some tests. Inform your doctor about medications or supplements you are taking.

Understanding Results

Elevated tumor marker levels may indicate cancer, but further tests are needed for a definitive diagnosis.
