Eye Care Overview

Ophthalmologists and optometrists provide complete eye care services including vision correction, disease treatment, and surgical procedures to maintain optimal eye health throughout your life.

Common Eye Conditions

  • Refractive Errors: Myopia, hyperopia, astigmatism, presbyopia
  • Cataracts: Clouding of the eye's natural lens
  • Glaucoma: Optic nerve damage from high eye pressure
  • Macular Degeneration: Deterioration of central vision
  • Diabetic Retinopathy: Diabetes-related retinal damage
  • Dry Eye Syndrome: Insufficient tear production

Eye Care Services

Our comprehensive eye care includes:

  1. Vision Correction: Glasses, contacts, and refractive surgery
  2. Medical Eye Care: Treatment for eye diseases and infections
  3. Surgical Procedures: Cataract, glaucoma, and retinal surgery
  4. Pediatric Eye Care: Specialized care for children's vision
  5. Low Vision Services: Support for visual impairment

Preventive Eye Care

  • Regular comprehensive eye exams (every 1-2 years for adults)
  • UV-protective sunglasses for outdoor activities
  • Proper nutrition with eye-healthy foods (leafy greens, fish)
  • Screen time management and the 20-20-20 rule
  • Proper contact lens hygiene and care
  • Smoking cessation to reduce eye disease risk
