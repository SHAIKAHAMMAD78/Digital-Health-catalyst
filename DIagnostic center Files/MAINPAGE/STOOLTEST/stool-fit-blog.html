Fecal Immunochemical Test (FIT)

What is a Fecal Immunochemical Test?

FIT is a non-invasive screening test that detects hidden blood in the stool, primarily used for colorectal cancer screening. It is more specific than traditional Fecal Occult Blood Tests (FOBT) and does not require dietary restrictions before testing.

How Does It Work?

A small stool sample is collected and analyzed for hemoglobin, a protein found in red blood cells. Unlike Guaiac-based FOBTs, FIT specifically detects human blood, reducing false positives caused by diet or medications.

Why is It Important?

Early detection of blood in stool can indicate colorectal cancer or other gastrointestinal issues. Regular FIT screenings help identify potential health concerns before symptoms appear, improving treatment success rates.

Benefits of FIT

FIT is simple, painless, and more accurate than older fecal blood tests. It requires no special diet and can be done conveniently at home, making it a preferred choice for routine colorectal cancer screening.

How to Prepare

FIT does not require dietary or medication restrictions before testing. Patients simply collect a stool sample using a provided kit and return it to the lab for analysis.

Understanding Results

A negative result means no blood was detected, while a positive result suggests the presence of blood, necessitating further examination such as a colonoscopy to determine the cause.
