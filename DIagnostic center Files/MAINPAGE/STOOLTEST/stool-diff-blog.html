Clostridium Difficile (C. diff) Toxin Test

What is a C. diff Toxin Test?

This test detects the presence of Clostridium difficile toxins in stool, helping diagnose C. diff infections.

How Does It Work?

A stool sample is tested for C. diff toxins using enzyme immunoassay (EIA) or PCR methods.

Why is It Important?

The test helps diagnose C. diff infections, which can cause severe diarrhea and colitis.

Benefits of a C. diff Toxin Test

Quick and accurate detection of infections, aiding in timely treatment.

How to Prepare

No special preparation is required, but a fresh stool sample is necessary for accurate results.

Understanding Results

Positive results indicate a C. diff infection that requires medical attention and possible antibiotic treatment.
