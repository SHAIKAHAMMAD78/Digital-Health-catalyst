Stool for Ova and Parasites (O&P) Test

What is a Stool for Ova and Parasites Test?

The O&P test is a laboratory analysis used to detect the presence of eggs (ova) and parasites in a stool sample, helping diagnose parasitic infections.

How Does It Work?

A stool sample is examined under a microscope to identify the presence of parasites or their eggs. Special stains and concentration techniques may be used to improve detection.

Why is It Important?

The test helps diagnose parasitic infections that cause gastrointestinal symptoms like diarrhea, cramps, and weight loss. Early detection is crucial for effective treatment.

Benefits of O&P Test

Provides accurate detection of parasites, guiding appropriate treatment. It is essential for travelers, immunocompromised individuals, and those experiencing prolonged gastrointestinal symptoms.

How to Prepare

Patients may need to avoid certain medications before the test. Multiple stool samples may be required for better accuracy.

Understanding Results

A positive result indicates the presence of parasites or ova, requiring targeted treatment. A negative result suggests no detectable infection at the time of testing.
