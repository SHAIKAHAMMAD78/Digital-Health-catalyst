Fecal Fat Test

What is a Fecal Fat Test?

This test measures the amount of fat in stool to evaluate digestive disorders and malabsorption conditions.

How Does It Work?

A stool sample is analyzed for fat content, which can indicate problems with digestion and nutrient absorption.

Why is It Important?

The test helps diagnose conditions like pancreatitis, celiac disease, and other malabsorption disorders.

Benefits of a Fecal Fat Test

Non-invasive, effective in diagnosing digestive issues, and helps guide treatment plans.

How to Prepare

Dietary restrictions may be required before the test, and stool samples are collected over a specific period.

Understanding Results

Elevated fat levels may indicate malabsorption disorders and require further medical evaluation.
