Fecal Occult Blood Test (FOBT)

What is a Fecal Occult Blood Test?

FOBT is a non-invasive screening test used to detect hidden blood in the stool, which may indicate conditions like colorectal cancer, ulcers, polyps, or gastrointestinal bleeding. It is commonly recommended for individuals over 50 as part of routine colorectal cancer screening.

How Does It Work?

A small stool sample is collected and tested for hidden blood using chemical or immunochemical methods. The most common types of FOBT include the Guaiac-based test (gFOBT) and the Fecal Immunochemical Test (FIT). These tests detect blood that is not visible to the naked eye.

Why is It Important?

Early detection of hidden blood in stool can help identify serious health conditions before symptoms appear. FOBT is a crucial tool for early diagnosis of colorectal cancer, allowing for timely intervention and treatment. Regular screening can significantly reduce mortality rates.

Benefits of FOBT

FOBT is simple, cost-effective, and non-invasive. It can be performed at home and requires minimal preparation. Unlike other diagnostic methods, FOBT does not require colon cleansing, making it a convenient choice for patients seeking routine screening.

How to Prepare

To improve accuracy, patients may need to avoid certain foods like red meat, vitamin C supplements, and certain medications before taking a Guaiac-based FOBT. Fecal Immunochemical Tests (FIT) typically do not require dietary restrictions.

Understanding Results

A negative result means no blood was detected, while a positive result indicates the presence of blood in the stool. Positive results do not confirm cancer but warrant further diagnostic tests such as a colonoscopy to determine the cause.
