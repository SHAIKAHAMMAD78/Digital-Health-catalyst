Stool pH Test

What is a Stool pH Test?

The stool pH test measures the acidity or alkalinity of a stool sample to help diagnose digestive disorders.

How Does It Work?

A stool sample is tested using pH indicators to determine its acid-base balance, which may indicate malabsorption or other issues.

Why is It Important?

It helps in diagnosing conditions like lactose intolerance, infections, and other gastrointestinal disorders.

Benefits of Stool pH Test

Provides a simple, non-invasive method to evaluate digestive health and detect abnormalities early.

How to Prepare

Avoid certain foods and medications that may alter stool pH before taking the test.

Understanding Results

A low pH indicates acidic stool, which may suggest malabsorption, while a high pH may be linked to bacterial overgrowth.
