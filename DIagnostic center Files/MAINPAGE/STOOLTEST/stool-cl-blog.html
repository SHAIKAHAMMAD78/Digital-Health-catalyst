Calprotectin and Lactoferrin Tests

What are Calprotectin and Lactoferrin Tests?

These tests measure specific proteins in stool that indicate inflammation in the intestines, helping to diagnose conditions like IBD.

How Do They Work?

Stool samples are analyzed for the presence of calprotectin and lactoferrin, which are released by white blood cells during inflammation.

Why Are They Important?

They help distinguish between inflammatory and non-inflammatory bowel conditions, reducing the need for invasive procedures like colonoscopy.

Benefits of Calprotectin and Lactoferrin Tests

Non-invasive, reliable, and effective in monitoring disease activity in patients with IBD.

How to Prepare

No special preparation is usually required, but avoiding NSAIDs and certain foods may be recommended.

Understanding Results

Elevated levels indicate inflammation, which may require further testing or treatment adjustments.
