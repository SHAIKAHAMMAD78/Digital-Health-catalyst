Health Risks Due to Allergies

Introduction

Allergies occur when the immune system overreacts to harmless substances, leading to various health issues. These reactions can range from mild irritation to severe anaphylaxis.

Common Allergy Risks

  • Hay Fever (Allergic Rhinitis): Causes sneezing, congestion, and itchy eyes due to pollen exposure.
  • Asthma: Allergic reactions can trigger asthma attacks, leading to breathing difficulties.
  • Food Allergies: Certain foods like nuts, shellfish, and dairy can cause severe reactions.
  • Skin Allergies: Conditions like eczema and hives result from allergic skin reactions.
  • Anaphylaxis: A life-threatening allergic reaction that requires immediate medical attention.
  • Insect Allergies: Bites or stings from bees and mosquitoes can trigger allergic responses.

How to Minimize Risks

  • Identify and avoid known allergens.
  • Use antihistamines and prescribed medications to manage symptoms.
  • Maintain a clean environment to reduce exposure to allergens like dust and pollen.
  • Carry an epinephrine auto-injector if you have severe allergies.
