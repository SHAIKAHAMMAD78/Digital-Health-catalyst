Urine Diagnostic Tests

What are Urine Tests?

Urine tests analyze samples to detect infections, kidney diseases, diabetes, and other metabolic disorders.

Common Urine Tests

  • Urinalysis: Detects abnormalities such as infections, kidney disease, and diabetes.
  • Urine Culture: Identifies bacteria or fungi causing urinary tract infections.
  • Protein Test: Measures protein levels to detect kidney dysfunction.
  • Ketone Test: Monitors ketone levels, often used for diabetes management.
  • Drug Screening: Detects the presence of drugs or substances in the urine.

Benefits of Urine Testing

  • Early detection of infections and kidney diseases
  • Monitoring metabolic conditions like diabetes
  • Screening for drug use
  • Assessing overall urinary tract health
  • Guiding treatment decisions
