Urine pH Test

What is a Urine pH Test?

A urine pH test measures the acidity or alkalinity of urine, helping to diagnose various health conditions.

How Does It Work?

A urine sample is tested using pH strips or laboratory analysis to determine its pH level.

Why is It Important?

It helps detect conditions like kidney disease, urinary tract infections, and metabolic disorders.

Benefits of Urine pH Test

Provides insights into metabolic health and helps in dietary adjustments.

How to Prepare

Avoid certain foods and medications that may alter urine pH before the test.

Understanding Results

pH levels below 4.5 indicate acidity, while above 8.0 indicate alkalinity, aiding in diagnosis.
