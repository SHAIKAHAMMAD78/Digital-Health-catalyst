Microalbumin Test

What is a Microalbumin Test?

A microalbumin test measures small amounts of albumin in urine to detect early kidney damage.

How Does It Work?

A urine sample is tested for albumin levels, helping assess kidney function, especially in diabetics.

Why is It Important?

This test helps diagnose early kidney disease before major symptoms appear.

Benefits of Microalbumin Test

It aids in monitoring kidney health, especially for individuals with diabetes or high blood pressure.

How to Prepare

Usually, no special preparation is needed, but hydration is recommended before sample collection.

Understanding Results

Results show albumin levels in urine, indicating kidney function and potential risk of disease.
