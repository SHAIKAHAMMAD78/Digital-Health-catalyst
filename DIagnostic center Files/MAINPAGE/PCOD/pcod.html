PCOD

Overview

PCOD (Polycystic Ovarian Disease) is diagnosed through a combination of medical history, blood tests, and ultrasound. Doctors look for irregular periods, elevated androgen levels, and the presence of multiple small cysts in the ovaries through a pelvic ultrasound. Blood tests check for hormone imbalances and insulin resistance. Symptoms like weight gain, acne, and excessive hair growth are also evaluated. Early detection of PCOD is important for managing symptoms and preventing complications such as diabetes and infertility.
