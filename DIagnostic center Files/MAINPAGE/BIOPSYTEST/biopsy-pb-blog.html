Punch Biopsy

What is a Punch Biopsy?

A punch biopsy is a procedure in which a small, circular tool is used to remove a deep skin sample for analysis.

How Does It Work?

A special punch tool is used to extract a full-thickness skin sample, which includes the epidermis, dermis, and superficial fat layers.

Why is It Important?

Punch biopsies help diagnose skin conditions such as melanoma, infections, and autoimmune disorders.

How to Prepare

Patients should avoid blood-thinning medications and discuss any allergies with their doctor before the procedure.

Recovery & Risks

Recovery is usually quick, with minimal pain. Risks include mild bleeding, infection, and scarring.
