Health Risks Due to Biopsy

Introduction

A biopsy is a medical procedure used to diagnose diseases by extracting tissue samples for examination. While essential for accurate diagnosis, biopsies carry certain risks and complications.

Common Biopsy-Related Risks

  • Bleeding: Some biopsies may lead to excessive bleeding, especially in individuals with clotting disorders.
  • Infection: Any invasive procedure carries the risk of infection at the biopsy site.
  • Pain & Discomfort: Patients may experience mild to severe pain after the procedure.
  • Scarring: Depending on the type of biopsy, minor scarring may occur.
  • Nerve Damage: In rare cases, biopsies performed near nerves can cause temporary or permanent nerve damage.
  • False Diagnosis: A biopsy sample may not always be representative, leading to misdiagnosis.

How to Minimize Risks

  • Follow pre-biopsy instructions carefully.
  • Choose a qualified and experienced medical professional.
  • Monitor the biopsy site for signs of infection, such as redness or swelling.
  • Inform your doctor about any medications that may affect bleeding.
