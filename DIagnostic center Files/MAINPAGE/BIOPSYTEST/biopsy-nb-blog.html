Needle Biopsy

What is a Needle Biopsy?

A needle biopsy is a procedure where a thin needle is used to extract tissue or fluid from an organ for examination. It is commonly used to diagnose cancer, infections, and other diseases.

How Does It Work?

A doctor inserts a fine or core needle into the targeted tissue, guided by imaging techniques like ultrasound or CT scans, to collect a sample for laboratory analysis.

Why is It Important?

Needle biopsies help diagnose conditions early, guide treatment plans, and monitor the effectiveness of ongoing treatments.

Types of Needle Biopsy

There are two main types: Fine-Needle Aspiration (FNA) and Core Needle Biopsy (CNB). FNA uses a thin needle, while CNB uses a larger needle for more tissue collection.

How to Prepare

Patients may need to stop taking blood thinners, avoid eating before the procedure, and follow specific instructions from their healthcare provider.

Recovery & Risks

Recovery is quick with minor discomfort. Risks include bleeding, infection, and rare complications depending on the biopsy site.
