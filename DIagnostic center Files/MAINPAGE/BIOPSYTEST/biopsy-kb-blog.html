Kidney Biopsy

What is a Kidney Biopsy?

A kidney biopsy is a medical procedure where a small sample of kidney tissue is removed for examination to diagnose kidney diseases.

How Does It Work?

A doctor uses a thin needle to extract a tissue sample from the kidney, which is then analyzed under a microscope to detect abnormalities.

Why is It Important?

Kidney biopsies help diagnose kidney disorders, assess damage, and determine the best treatment plans.
