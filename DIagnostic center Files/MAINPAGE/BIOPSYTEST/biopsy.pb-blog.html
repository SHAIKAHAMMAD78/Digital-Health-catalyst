Prostate Biopsy

What is a Prostate Biopsy?

A prostate biopsy is a procedure in which small samples of prostate tissue are removed to check for prostate cancer or other abnormalities.

How Does It Work?

A needle is inserted through the rectum or perineum to collect small tissue samples, which are then examined under a microscope.

Why is It Important?

Prostate biopsies are essential in diagnosing prostate cancer, guiding treatment decisions, and assessing prostate health.

How to Prepare

Patients may need to stop blood thinners, take antibiotics, and perform an enema before the procedure to reduce infection risks.

Recovery & Risks

Recovery includes monitoring for bleeding, infection, and temporary discomfort. Patients should avoid strenuous activity for a few days.
