Excisional & Incisional Biopsy

What are Excisional & Incisional Biopsies?

Excisional biopsy involves removing an entire lump or abnormal tissue, while incisional biopsy removes only a portion for examination.

How Do They Work?

Both procedures require a surgical incision. Excisional biopsy removes the whole lesion, whereas incisional biopsy collects a sample for analysis.

Why are They Important?

These biopsies help diagnose cancer, infections, and inflammatory diseases by providing a larger tissue sample for accurate analysis.

How to Prepare

Patients may need to avoid certain medications, fast before the procedure, and discuss anesthesia options with their doctor.

Recovery & Risks

Recovery varies depending on the biopsy type. Risks include infection, bleeding, and scarring, but complications are rare with proper care.
