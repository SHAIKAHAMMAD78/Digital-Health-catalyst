Understanding Blood Health Risks

What Are Blood-Related Health Risks?

Blood disorders can range from mild conditions like anemia to life-threatening diseases such as leukemia, hemophilia, and thrombosis.

Common Blood Disorders

  • Anemia: A condition with insufficient healthy red blood cells, causing fatigue and weakness.
  • Hemophilia: A rare disorder where blood does not clot properly, leading to excessive bleeding.
  • Blood Clots: Clots can block blood flow, leading to strokes or heart attacks.
  • Leukemia: A type of cancer affecting blood-forming tissues.
  • Sickle Cell Disease: A genetic disorder causing misshapen red blood cells.

Causes & Risk Factors

  • Genetic predisposition
  • Poor diet lacking iron, vitamin B12, or folate
  • Chronic diseases such as diabetes
  • Exposure to toxins or radiation
  • Infections affecting the blood

Symptoms to Watch For

  • Unexplained fatigue and weakness
  • Frequent infections
  • Excessive bruising or prolonged bleeding
  • Pale or yellowish skin
  • Shortness of breath and dizziness
