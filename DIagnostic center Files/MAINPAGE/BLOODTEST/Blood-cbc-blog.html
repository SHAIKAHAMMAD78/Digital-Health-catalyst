Complete Blood Count (CBC)

What is a Complete Blood Count (CBC)?

The Complete Blood Count (CBC) test is a blood test used to evaluate overall health and detect a variety of disorders, such as anemia, infection, and leukemia.

How Does It Work?

A blood sample is taken from a vein and analyzed to measure different components, including red and white blood cells, hemoglobin, and platelets.

Why is It Important?

The CBC test helps diagnose infections, anemia, and other medical conditions, providing critical information about a patientâ€™s health.

Benefits of CBC Test

The test is a simple yet effective way to monitor health, detect diseases early, and guide treatment decisions.

How to Prepare

Generally, no special preparation is needed, but some tests may require fasting. Consult your doctor for specific instructions.

Understanding Results

Results show the levels of blood cells and can indicate infections, clotting disorders, or other health issues that may require further investigation.
