Holter Monitoring

What is Holter Monitoring?

Holter monitoring is a continuous ECG recording over 24-48 hours to detect irregular heart rhythms.

How Does It Work?

A portable ECG device records heart activity as the patient goes about their daily routine.

Why is It Important?

It helps diagnose intermittent arrhythmias and unexplained dizziness or palpitations.

Types of Holter Monitors

Includes 24-hour Holter monitors, extended event recorders, and wireless Holter monitors.

How to Prepare

Wear loose clothing, avoid getting the device wet, and keep a diary of symptoms and activities.

Understanding Results

Results help doctors determine the presence of arrhythmias or heart abnormalities requiring treatment.
