Stress Test (Treadmill Test or Exercise ECG)

What is a Stress Test?

A stress test evaluates heart function during physical activity, typically using a treadmill or stationary bike.

How Does It Work?

The patient exercises while heart activity is monitored through an ECG to assess heart response.

Why is It Important?

It helps diagnose heart conditions, evaluate blood flow, and assess exercise tolerance.

Types of Stress Tests

Includes treadmill stress test, nuclear stress test, and stress echocardiogram.

How to Prepare

Wear comfortable clothing, avoid caffeine before the test, and follow the doctor's instructions.

Understanding Results

Results help doctors determine if further testing or treatment is needed for heart conditions.
