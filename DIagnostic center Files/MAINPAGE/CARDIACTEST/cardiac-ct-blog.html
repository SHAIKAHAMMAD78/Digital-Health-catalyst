Cardiac CT Scan

What is a Cardiac CT Scan?

A cardiac CT scan uses X-ray technology to create detailed images of the heart and blood vessels.

How Does It Work?

A special dye is injected into the bloodstream, and a CT scanner captures detailed cross-sectional images of the heart.

Why is It Important?

It helps detect coronary artery disease, blockages, and calcium deposits in the arteries.

Types of Cardiac CT Scans

Includes coronary calcium scoring and CT angiography to assess heart and vascular health.

How to Prepare

Avoid caffeine and smoking before the test, and inform the doctor of any allergies to contrast dye.

Understanding Results

Doctors analyze the images to determine the presence of heart disease, narrowing arteries, or calcium buildup.
