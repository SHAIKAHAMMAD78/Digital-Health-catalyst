Echocardiogram (Echo)

What is an Echocardiogram?

An echocardiogram (Echo) is a test that uses ultrasound waves to create images of the heart.

How Does It Work?

Ultrasound waves are sent through the chest to produce images of the heartâ€™s structure and function.

Why is It Important?

Echo tests help diagnose heart diseases, valve problems, and monitor heart conditions.

Types of Echocardiograms

Includes transthoracic, transesophageal, stress echocardiograms, and Doppler ultrasounds.

How to Prepare

Generally, no special preparation is needed, but fasting may be required for certain types.

Understanding Results

Doctors analyze Echo images to assess heart function and detect abnormalities.
