Sigmoidoscopy

What is a Sigmoidoscopy?

Sigmoidoscopy is a procedure used to examine the lower part of the colon for abnormalities.

How Does a Sigmoidoscopy Work?

A flexible tube with a camera is inserted into the rectum to inspect the sigmoid colon for polyps or disease.

Why is Sigmoidoscopy Important?

It helps detect colorectal cancer, polyps, and inflammatory bowel diseases at an early stage.

Understanding the Results

Results can reveal colon abnormalities like tumors, ulcers, or inflammation.

How to Prepare

Patients should follow dietary restrictions and bowel-cleansing instructions before the procedure.

Benefits of Sigmoidoscopy

Sigmoidoscopy allows early detection of colorectal issues, helping in timely intervention and treatment.
