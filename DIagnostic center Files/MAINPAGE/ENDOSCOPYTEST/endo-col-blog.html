Colonoscopy

What is a Colonoscopy?

A colonoscopy is a procedure used to examine the inner lining of the colon and rectum.

How Does a Colonoscopy Work?

A flexible tube with a camera is inserted through the rectum to inspect the large intestine.

Why is Colonoscopy Important?

Colonoscopy helps detect colorectal cancer, polyps, and other digestive disorders.

Understanding the Results

Results can reveal inflammation, abnormal growths, or other digestive tract issues.

How to Prepare

Patients need to follow a special diet and take prescribed bowel-cleansing solutions before the procedure.

Benefits of Colonoscopy

Colonoscopy allows early detection of colorectal cancer and removal of polyps before they become malignant.
