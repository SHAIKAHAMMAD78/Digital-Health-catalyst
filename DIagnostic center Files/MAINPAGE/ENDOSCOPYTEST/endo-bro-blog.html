Bronchoscopy

What is a Bronchoscopy?

A bronchoscopy is a procedure used to examine the airways and lungs using a thin, flexible tube with a camera.

How Does a Bronchoscopy Work?

A bronchoscope is inserted through the nose or mouth into the lungs to inspect airways and collect tissue samples if needed.

Why is Bronchoscopy Important?

Bronchoscopy helps diagnose lung diseases, infections, and tumors by providing a direct view of the airways.

Understanding the Results

Results can help detect lung infections, inflammation, cancer, or blockages in the airways.

How to Prepare

Patients should fast for several hours before the procedure and inform their doctor about any medications.

Benefits of Bronchoscopy

Bronchoscopy aids in early disease detection, precise lung biopsy, and clearing airway obstructions.
