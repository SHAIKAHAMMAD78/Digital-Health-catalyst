Understanding Endoscopy

What Is Endoscopy?

Endoscopy is a medical procedure that uses a flexible tube with a camera to examine the digestive tract or other internal organs. It helps diagnose and treat various conditions without the need for invasive surgery.

Types of Endoscopy

  • Gastroscopy: Examines the esophagus, stomach, and upper small intestine.
  • Colonoscopy: Evaluates the large intestine and rectum for abnormalities.
  • Bronchoscopy: Used to examine the airways and lungs.
  • Cystoscopy: Inspects the bladder and urethra.
  • Laparoscopy: A minimally invasive surgical procedure to view the abdominal organs.

Why Is Endoscopy Performed?

  • To investigate symptoms like persistent stomach pain, difficulty swallowing, or abnormal bleeding.
  • To diagnose conditions such as ulcers, infections, or tumors.
  • To monitor ongoing medical conditions.
  • To remove polyps, take tissue samples (biopsies), or treat certain conditions.

Preparation & Recovery

  • Fasting is usually required for several hours before the procedure.
  • A sedative may be administered to keep the patient comfortable.
  • Recovery time is usually short, but patients may need someone to drive them home.
  • Follow-up instructions will be provided based on the findings.
