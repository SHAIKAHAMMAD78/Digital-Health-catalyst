Upper Endoscopy (EGD)

What is Upper Endoscopy (EGD)?

Upper endoscopy (EGD) is a procedure used to examine the esophagus, stomach, and the first part of the small intestine.

How Does EGD Work?

A thin, flexible tube with a camera is inserted through the mouth to inspect the digestive tract.

Why is EGD Important?

EGD helps diagnose conditions like ulcers, reflux disease, and gastrointestinal bleeding.

Understanding the Results

Results can indicate inflammation, infections, or abnormal growths in the digestive tract.

How to Prepare

Patients should avoid eating or drinking for several hours before the procedure.

Benefits of EGD

EGD provides a clear view of the upper digestive tract and allows for biopsies if needed.
