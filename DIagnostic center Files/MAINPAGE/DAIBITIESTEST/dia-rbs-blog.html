Random Blood Sugar (RBS)

What is Random Blood Sugar (RBS)?

Random Blood Sugar (RBS) is a test that measures blood glucose levels at any time of the day, regardless of when you last ate.

How Does It Work?

A blood sample is taken at a random time to check glucose levels, helping detect diabetes and monitor blood sugar fluctuations.

Why is It Important?

RBS is essential for diagnosing diabetes, monitoring glucose control, and identifying immediate blood sugar imbalances.

How to Prepare

No special preparation is required for an RBS test, as it can be taken at any time, regardless of food intake.

Understanding Results

Normal RBS levels typically range below 140 mg/dL. Higher levels may indicate diabetes or the need for further testing.
