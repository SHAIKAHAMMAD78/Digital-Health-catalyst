Fasting Blood Sugar (FBS)

What is Fasting Blood Sugar (FBS)?

Fasting Blood Sugar (FBS) is a test that measures blood glucose levels after an overnight fast. It helps diagnose diabetes and monitor glucose levels.

How Does It Work?

A blood sample is taken in the morning before eating to measure glucose levels, which indicate how the body regulates blood sugar.

Why is It Important?

FBS is crucial for detecting prediabetes, diabetes, and insulin resistance, which can lead to severe health complications if untreated.

How to Prepare

Avoid eating or drinking anything except water for at least 8 hours before the test to ensure accurate results.

Understanding Results

Normal FBS levels are typically between 70-100 mg/dL. Higher levels may indicate prediabetes or diabetes and require further evaluation.
