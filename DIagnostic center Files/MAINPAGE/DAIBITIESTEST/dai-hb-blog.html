HbA1c (Glycated Hemoglobin Test)

What is HbA1c?

HbA1c, or glycated hemoglobin, is a blood test that measures average blood sugar levels over the past 2-3 months.

How Does It Work?

The test measures the percentage of hemoglobin in your blood that is coated with sugar, indicating long-term glucose control.

Why is It Important?

HbA1c helps diagnose diabetes, monitor blood sugar control, and assess the risk of diabetes-related complications.

How to Prepare

No fasting is required for an HbA1c test, making it a convenient option for monitoring long-term glucose levels.

Understanding Results

Normal HbA1c levels are below 5.7%. Levels between 5.7% and 6.4% indicate prediabetes, while 6.5% or higher suggests diabetes.
