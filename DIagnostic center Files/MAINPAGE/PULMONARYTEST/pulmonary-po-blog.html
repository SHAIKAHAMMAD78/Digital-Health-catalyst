Pulse Oximetry

What is Pulse Oximetry?

Pulse oximetry is a non-invasive test that measures oxygen saturation levels in the blood.

How Does Pulse Oximetry Work?

A small device, called a pulse oximeter, clips onto a fingertip or earlobe to measure oxygen levels and heart rate.

Why is Pulse Oximetry Important?

It helps detect low oxygen levels, monitor respiratory conditions, and assess overall lung function.

Understanding the Results

Normal oxygen saturation levels range between 95% and 100%, while lower readings may indicate breathing difficulties.

How to Prepare

Ensure fingers are clean and free from nail polish, which can interfere with readings.

Benefits of Pulse Oximetry

It provides a quick, painless way to assess oxygen levels, especially for those with respiratory issues.
