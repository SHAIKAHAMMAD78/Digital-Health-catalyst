Pulmonary Function Tests (PFTs)

What are Pulmonary Function Tests?

Pulmonary Function Tests (PFTs) are a group of tests that measure how well the lungs are working by evaluating lung volume, capacity, and airflow.

How Do PFTs Work?

PFTs involve breathing into specialized devices that measure lung function, including spirometry, plethysmography, and gas diffusion tests.

Why Are PFTs Important?

PFTs help diagnose respiratory conditions like asthma, COPD, pulmonary fibrosis, and other lung diseases.

Understanding the Results

PFT results help determine lung health, airflow restrictions, and guide doctors in treatment and management.

How to Prepare

Avoid smoking, large meals, and caffeine before the test. Wear loose clothing and follow your doctor's instructions.

Benefits of PFTs

PFTs are non-invasive, provide vital information about lung function, and help manage chronic respiratory conditions effectively.
