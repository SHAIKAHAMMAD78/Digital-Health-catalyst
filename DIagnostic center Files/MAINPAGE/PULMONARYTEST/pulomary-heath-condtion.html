Pulmonary Diagnostic Tests

What are Pulmonary Tests?

Pulmonary tests assess lung function and help diagnose conditions such as asthma, COPD, and lung infections. These tests measure airflow, lung volume, and oxygen levels.

Common Pulmonary Tests

  • Spirometry: Measures airflow and detects conditions like asthma and COPD.
  • Chest X-ray: Provides images of the lungs to detect infections or abnormalities.
  • CT Scan: Offers detailed lung images for diagnosing diseases like lung cancer.
  • Pulse Oximetry: Measures oxygen levels in the blood.
  • Bronchoscopy: Uses a camera to examine the airways and collect tissue samples.

Benefits of Pulmonary Testing

  • Early detection of lung diseases
  • Monitoring respiratory conditions
  • Guiding treatment options
  • Assessing lung function in chronic conditions
  • Improving overall respiratory health
