Chest X-ray

What is a Chest X-ray?

A Chest X-ray is a diagnostic imaging test that uses X-ray beams to create images of the lungs, heart, and chest structures.

How Does a Chest X-ray Work?

During a Chest X-ray, a small amount of radiation passes through the chest to capture images of internal structures.

Why Are Chest X-rays Important?

Chest X-rays help diagnose conditions like pneumonia, lung disease, heart failure, and fractures of the ribs.

Understanding the Results

Radiologists analyze the images to detect abnormalities, lung infections, tumors, and other health issues.

How to Prepare

Remove jewelry, wear loose clothing, and inform the technician if you are pregnant before the test.

Benefits of Chest X-rays

They are quick, non-invasive, painless, and provide critical information about lung and heart health.
