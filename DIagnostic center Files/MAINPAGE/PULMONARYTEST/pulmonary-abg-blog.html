Arterial Blood Gas (ABG) Test

What is an ABG Test?

An Arterial Blood Gas (ABG) test measures oxygen, carbon dioxide, and pH levels in arterial blood to assess lung function and acid-base balance.

How is the ABG Test Performed?

A sample of arterial blood is taken, usually from the wrist, and analyzed for gas exchange efficiency and acid-base status.

Why is an ABG Test Important?

The ABG test helps diagnose respiratory conditions, metabolic disorders, and assess the effectiveness of oxygen therapy.

Understanding the Results

ABG results provide critical insights into oxygenation, ventilation, and acid-base imbalances, guiding treatment decisions.

How to Prepare

There are minimal preparations, but patients should inform their doctor about medications that may affect blood gas levels.

Benefits of ABG Test

ABG tests provide crucial information for managing conditions like COPD, asthma, and critical care scenarios.
