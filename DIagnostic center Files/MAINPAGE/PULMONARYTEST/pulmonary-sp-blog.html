Spirometry

What is Spirometry?

Spirometry is a pulmonary function test that measures how much and how quickly you can move air in and out of your lungs.

How Does It Work?

During the test, a patient breathes into a mouthpiece connected to a spirometer, which records lung function measurements.

Why Is It Important?

Spirometry helps diagnose conditions like asthma, COPD, and other lung diseases, ensuring timely treatment.

Understanding the Results

Results indicate lung capacity, airflow obstruction, and respiratory health, guiding doctors in treatment decisions.

How to Prepare

Avoid smoking, caffeine, and heavy meals before the test. Follow your doctor's instructions carefully.

Benefits of Spirometry

Spirometry is a quick, non-invasive test that provides critical insights into lung function and respiratory health.
