CT Scan (Chest Computed Tomography)

What is a Chest CT Scan?

A Chest CT Scan (Computed Tomography) is a diagnostic imaging test that provides detailed cross-sectional images of the lungs and chest.

How Does a CT Scan Work?

A CT scanner uses X-rays and a computer to create high-resolution images of the chest from different angles.

Why is a CT Scan Important?

CT scans help detect lung diseases, infections, tumors, blood clots, and other abnormalities in the chest area.

Understanding the Results

CT scan results provide detailed insights into lung and chest conditions, helping doctors make accurate diagnoses.

How to Prepare

Patients may be asked to avoid eating or drinking before the scan and inform their doctor about any allergies or medical conditions.

Benefits of CT Scan

CT scans provide fast, detailed images, aiding in early detection and effective treatment planning for various chest conditions.
