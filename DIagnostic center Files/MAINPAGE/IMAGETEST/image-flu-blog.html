Fluoroscopy

What is Fluoroscopy?

Fluoroscopy is a medical imaging technique that provides real-time X-ray images of internal structures.

How Does It Work?

A continuous X-ray beam passes through the body, creating dynamic images on a monitor.

Why is It Important?

Fluoroscopy is used in diagnostic and interventional procedures, such as catheter placements and barium studies.

Benefits of Fluoroscopy

Provides real-time imaging for accurate diagnosis and guidance during medical procedures.

How to Prepare

Preparation depends on the procedure and may involve fasting or contrast ingestion.

Understanding Results

Radiologists interpret the images to diagnose conditions and guide treatments.
