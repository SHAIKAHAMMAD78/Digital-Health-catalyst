Endoscopy & Capsule Endoscopy

What is Endoscopy?

Endoscopy is a medical procedure that allows doctors to view the inside of the body using a flexible tube with a camera.

How Does It Work?

A thin, flexible tube called an endoscope is inserted into the body to capture detailed images of internal organs.

Why is It Important?

Endoscopy helps diagnose and treat conditions affecting the digestive system, lungs, and other organs.

Capsule Endoscopy

Capsule endoscopy involves swallowing a small camera pill that takes images of the digestive tract.

How to Prepare

Patients may need to fast and stop certain medications before the procedure.

Understanding Results

Doctors analyze the images to detect abnormalities and recommend further treatment if necessary.
