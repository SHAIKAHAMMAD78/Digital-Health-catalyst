Positron Emission Tomography (PET Scan)

What is a PET Scan?

A PET scan is an imaging test that helps reveal how tissues and organs function using a radioactive tracer.

How Does It Work?

A small amount of radioactive tracer is injected into the body, and the scanner detects signals to create detailed images.

Why is It Important?

PET scans are used to detect cancer, heart disease, and brain disorders by analyzing metabolic activity.

Benefits of PET Scan

Provides early detection of diseases, assesses treatment effectiveness, and helps guide medical decisions.

How to Prepare

Patients may need to fast for several hours and avoid strenuous activities before the test.

Understanding Results

Doctors analyze the scan to detect abnormalities in metabolic activity, which may indicate disease.
