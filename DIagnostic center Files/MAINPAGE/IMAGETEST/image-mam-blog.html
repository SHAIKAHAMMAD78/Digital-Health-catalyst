Mammography

What is Mammography?

Mammography is a specialized medical imaging technique that uses low-dose X-rays to examine breast tissue for signs of abnormalities or cancer.

How Does It Work?

During a mammogram, the breast is compressed between two plates to spread the tissue and capture clear X-ray images.

Why is It Important?

Mammography is crucial for early detection of breast cancer, increasing the chances of successful treatment.

Benefits of Mammography

Allows for early detection of tumors and abnormalities, helping reduce mortality rates from breast cancer.

How to Prepare

Avoid using deodorants or lotions on the day of the exam, and wear a two-piece outfit for convenience.

Understanding Results

Radiologists analyze the mammogram images to detect any suspicious areas that may require further testing.
