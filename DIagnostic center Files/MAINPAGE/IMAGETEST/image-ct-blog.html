Computed Tomography (CT Scan)

What is a CT Scan?

A CT scan is a diagnostic imaging technique that uses X-rays and computer processing to create detailed cross-sectional images of the body.

How Does It Work?

A rotating X-ray machine takes multiple images, which are then processed by a computer to generate detailed 3D visuals of internal structures.

Why is It Important?

CT scans help diagnose diseases, detect injuries, and guide medical procedures with precision.

Benefits of CT Scan

Provides fast, detailed imaging, aiding in early diagnosis and effective treatment planning.

How to Prepare

Patients may need to avoid food, drink contrast dye, or wear loose clothing for accurate imaging.

Understanding Results

Radiologists analyze CT images to detect abnormalities and provide insights for diagnosis and treatment.
