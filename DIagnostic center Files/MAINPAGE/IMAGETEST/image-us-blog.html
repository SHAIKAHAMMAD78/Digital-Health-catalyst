Ultrasounds

What is an Ultrasound?

Ultrasound is a medical imaging technique that uses high-frequency sound waves to capture live images from inside the body.

How Does It Work?

A transducer sends sound waves into the body, and the echoes create real-time images of organs and tissues.

Why is It Important?

Ultrasounds help in diagnosing medical conditions, monitoring pregnancies, and guiding procedures.

Benefits of Ultrasound

It is a non-invasive, radiation-free imaging method with real-time visualization.

How to Prepare

Preparation varies by type; some require fasting, while others need a full bladder.

Understanding Results

Doctors analyze ultrasound images to diagnose conditions and plan treatments.
