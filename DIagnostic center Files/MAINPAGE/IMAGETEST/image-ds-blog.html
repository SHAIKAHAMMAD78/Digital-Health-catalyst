DEXA Scan (Bone Density Scan)

What is a DEXA Scan?

A DEXA scan measures bone mineral density to assess bone health and the risk of fractures.

How Does It Work?

DEXA uses low-dose X-rays to analyze bone density, usually in the spine and hips.

Why is It Important?

It helps diagnose osteoporosis and assess fracture risk, especially in older adults.

Benefits of DEXA Scan

Non-invasive, quick, and provides precise measurements of bone health.

How to Prepare

Avoid calcium supplements 24 hours before the scan and wear loose clothing.

Understanding Results

Results are given as T-scores and Z-scores, indicating bone strength compared to normal values.
