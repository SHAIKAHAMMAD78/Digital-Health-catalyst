Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)

What is an MRI?

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) is a medical imaging technique that uses strong magnetic fields and radio waves to create detailed images of organs and tissues.

How Does It Work?

MRI scanners use powerful magnets to align hydrogen atoms in the body. Radio waves then disturb this alignment, and the signals emitted are captured to form detailed images.

Why is It Important?

MRI is a non-invasive imaging technique used for diagnosing conditions affecting soft tissues, the brain, spinal cord, and joints.

Benefits of MRI

Provides high-resolution images without radiation exposure, making it safe for repeated use.

How to Prepare

Patients should remove metal objects, inform doctors about implants, and follow specific guidelines for contrast-enhanced scans.

Understanding Results

Radiologists interpret MRI scans to diagnose conditions and guide treatment plans.
