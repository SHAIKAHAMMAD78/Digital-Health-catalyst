Understanding Medical Imaging Tests

What Are Medical Imaging Tests?

Medical imaging tests help doctors diagnose and monitor conditions inside the body using various technologies, such as X-rays, MRIs, and ultrasounds.

Types of Imaging Tests

  • X-ray: Uses radiation to capture images of bones and internal organs.
  • MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging): Uses powerful magnets and radio waves to create detailed images of soft tissues and organs.
  • CT Scan (Computed Tomography): Combines X-rays and computer processing to create cross-sectional images.
  • Ultrasound: Uses sound waves to create real-time images, commonly used for pregnancy and organ imaging.
  • PET Scan (Positron Emission Tomography): Detects metabolic activity and helps in cancer diagnosis.

When Are Imaging Tests Needed?

  • Detecting fractures, tumors, or infections
  • Monitoring chronic conditions like arthritis or heart disease
  • Evaluating organ health and function
  • Guiding surgical procedures
  • Screening for early signs of disease
