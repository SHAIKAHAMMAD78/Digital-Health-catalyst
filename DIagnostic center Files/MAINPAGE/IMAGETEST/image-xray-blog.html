X-ray

What is an X-ray?

X-rays are a form of electromagnetic radiation used to create images of the inside of the body.

How Does It Work?

X-rays pass through the body and are absorbed at different rates, producing an image on a detector.

Why is It Important?

X-rays are crucial for diagnosing fractures, infections, and other medical conditions.

Benefits of X-ray

Provides fast and non-invasive imaging, aiding in quick diagnosis and treatment.

How to Prepare

Wear comfortable clothing and remove metal objects to ensure clear imaging.

Understanding Results

Doctors analyze X-ray images to diagnose conditions and plan appropriate treatments.
