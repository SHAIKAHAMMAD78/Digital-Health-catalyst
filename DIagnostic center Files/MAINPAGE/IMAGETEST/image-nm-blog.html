Nuclear Medicine Scans

What are Nuclear Medicine Scans?

Nuclear medicine scans use small amounts of radioactive materials to diagnose and treat various conditions.

How Do They Work?

A radiotracer is injected, inhaled, or swallowed, and special cameras capture images of the targeted organ.

Why Are They Important?

They help in diagnosing cancer, heart disease, and other conditions at an early stage.

Benefits of Nuclear Medicine

Provides detailed functional imaging and helps in targeted treatments with minimal invasiveness.

How to Prepare

Preparation varies by scan type and may include fasting or avoiding certain medications.

Understanding Results

Radiologists analyze the images to detect abnormalities and guide further treatment.
