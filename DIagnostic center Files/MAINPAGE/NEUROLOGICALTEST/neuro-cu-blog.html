Carotid Ultrasound

What is a Carotid Ultrasound?

A carotid ultrasound is a non-invasive imaging test that uses sound waves to examine the carotid arteries in the neck.

How is it Performed?

A small handheld device called a transducer is placed on the neck to capture images of the carotid arteries.

Why is it Important?

It helps detect blockages or narrowing in the carotid arteries, which can lead to strokes.

Are There Any Risks?

There are no known risks, as it is a safe and painless procedure.

How to Prepare

Patients may be asked to avoid heavy meals before the test and to wear loose-fitting clothing.

Understanding Results

The test helps determine if there is a risk of stroke due to narrowed or blocked arteries.
