EEG (Electroencephalogram)

What is an EEG?

An EEG (Electroencephalogram) is a test that measures electrical activity in the brain using small electrodes attached to the scalp.

How Does It Work?

Electrodes placed on the scalp detect brainwave activity, which is recorded and analyzed for abnormalities.

Why is It Important?

EEGs help diagnose conditions like epilepsy, sleep disorders, and brain dysfunctions.

Types of EEG

Includes routine EEG, ambulatory EEG, video EEG monitoring, and sleep EEG.

How to Prepare

Avoid caffeine before the test and ensure the scalp is clean for better electrode contact.

Understanding Results

EEG results indicate normal or abnormal brain activity, helping guide further medical evaluations.
