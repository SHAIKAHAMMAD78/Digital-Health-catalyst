Neurological Diagnostic Tests

What are Neurological Tests?

Neurological tests are used to diagnose conditions affecting the brain, spinal cord, and nerves. These tests help identify disorders such as epilepsy, stroke, and multiple sclerosis.

Common Neurological Tests

  • EEG (Electroencephalogram): Measures electrical activity in the brain to diagnose epilepsy and sleep disorders.
  • MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging): Provides detailed images of the brain and spinal cord.
  • CT Scan: Used for detecting strokes, brain injuries, and tumors.
  • EMG (Electromyography): Assesses nerve and muscle function.
  • Nerve Conduction Study: Tests how well nerves transmit electrical signals.

Benefits of Neurological Testing

  • Early detection of brain and nerve disorders
  • Accurate diagnosis of neurological conditions
  • Monitoring disease progression
  • Guiding treatment plans
  • Improving patient outcomes
